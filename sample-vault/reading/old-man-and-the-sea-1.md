# Old Man and the Sea — the opening

Santiago has gone 84 days without a fish. The other fishermen pity him or laugh at
him; only the boy, Manolin, still believes in him. Hemingway treats luck as almost
physical — the old man has simply run out of it, so he rows far out, alone, to find
it again.

The prose is stripped bare. Almost no adjectives. The restraint *is* the point.
