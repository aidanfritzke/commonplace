# Why I Keep a Commonplace Book

A commonplace book is not a diary. A diary records what happened; a commonplace book records what is *worth keeping* — a quotation, an argument, a turn of phrase, a question you don't yet have the answer to. The point is accumulation toward understanding.

The practice is old. Renaissance scholars kept them; so did Locke, who wrote a treatise on how to index one. The modern version is a folder of plain text files: durable, searchable, yours.

What journaling and Stoic practice share is the morning return to first principles — writing not to perform but to think. The page is where judgment gets rehearsed before the day tests it.
