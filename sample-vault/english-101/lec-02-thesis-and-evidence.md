# ENG 101 — Lecture 2 — Thesis & evidence

A thesis is a claim a reasonable person could disagree with. "Hemingway uses simple
prose" is not a thesis; "Hemingway's plainness forces the reader to supply the
feeling" is.

- Each paragraph = one claim + evidence + so-what.
- Quote sparingly. The analysis is yours, not the quotation's.
- A counterargument makes you stronger, not weaker — address it head-on.
