# Old Man and the Sea — the marlin

Day two on the line. The fish is bigger than the skiff, and Santiago can't see it —
only feel it through the cord cutting his hands. He talks to it, calls it brother,
even as he means to kill it: *"Fish, I love you and respect you very much. But I
will kill you before this day ends."*

The struggle is mutual. Neither is winning; both are enduring. The dignity is in
the contest, not the victory.
