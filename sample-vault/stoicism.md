# On Stoicism

The Stoics held that the only thing fully in our control is our own judgment.
Externals — reputation, wealth, health, the actions of others — are "preferred"
or "dispreferred" but never *good* or *bad* in themselves. What is good is a
well-governed mind; what is bad is a mind enslaved to passion.

Marcus Aurelius wrote his *Meditations* to no audience but himself. They read as
reminders, not arguments: rise early, do the work of a human being, do not be
disturbed by what you cannot change. The discipline is daily, not theoretical.

Epictetus, born a slave, drew the sharpest line: "It is not things that disturb
us, but our judgments about things." The promise is not a life without loss but
a mind that loss cannot capsize.
